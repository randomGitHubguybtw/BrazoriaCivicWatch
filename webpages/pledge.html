Here at the Brazoria Civic Watch, we take transparency seriously. In fact, its written into our bylaws!

Bylaws financial integrity section:

No member of The Board nor the House of the Presidents may take money that is considered “dark money,” such as corporate PACs or PACs that benefit or favor one policy, country, or candidate over another; 501(c)(4) groups, or independent expenditure committees are also banned from donations to specific members of either chamber [of Brazoria Civic Watch governance]. Such organizations may donate to the Brazoria Civic Watch itself; however, the donations must be taken just as is, with no deal, incentive, or anything transactional between the organization and the Brazoria Civic Watch or any of its members of either chamber [of Brazoria Civic Watch governance].

The Brazoria Civic Watch may not use any space on any platform representing the Brazoria Civic Watch, including volunteers, website, employees, and more, to advertise or otherwise endorse any entity, whether it be political party, organization, corporation, individual, or political candidate; save for accidental exposure, in which if a volunteer or representative of the like is unable to use unbranded products, as long as no acknowledgement or endorsement is made, and the item is used only when necessary; this exception does not apply to the camera, in which unbranded or ambiguously branded items, for representatives of Brazoria Civic Watch, are required.

The Brazoria Civic Watch pledges to be non-partisan, transparent, reliable and uncompromising in these roles. Candidate interviews must be held only after every member of the specific race has been asked an interview. Candidates are disallowed from knowing anything about the content of their interviews beforehand, except the following Candidates may know that the interview will be relevant to themselves as a political candidate. Candidates may know that the interview will be presented in a non-partisan fashion, with questions non-partisan in nature. Candidates may know that the interview, while containing nuance and nuanced questions, will not contain arbitrary questions or “gotcha questions." The Brazoria Civic Watch will keep its information, including election information, sitting officials information, and local meeting summary information as up to date as possible by the ability of the volunteer force.

With that being said, it means that here at the Brazoria Civic Watch, we will not endorse any political party, corporation, candidate or entity. We as an organization are solely independent, and will not force you to pay any money nor will we get paid to advertise or secretly support some candidate, party, or corporation! We are nonpartisan and solely for the people of Brazoria County.

Any entity that tries to make a backdoor deal or a transactional donation can be found on the Donor Wall of Shame!
